The Diamond Data Example

An annotated notebook (right-click and Download as Diamonds/Diamonds.ipynb) has been prepared, based on existing published notebooks but with enhancements a) to include new features and b) to provide extra explanations of what is happening.

The workbook includes two short exercises:

  1. Mean-centre the data and interpret the results
  2. Display the residuals and check certain properties graphically

Note that centring data by its mean is generally good advice

  • Some analyses make sense only with mean-centred and/or normalised data;
  • Generally, mean-centred data has better numerical properties, making statistical procedures more precise.
